Flagship · Final Year Thesis
Multimodal Breast Cancer Detection
PyTorch • MedCLIP • TabNet • Early Fusion • Landmark University 2024–2025

Addressed single-modality screening limitations by designing a fusion architecture combining ultrasound imaging with clinical and radiometric data. 87.1% ROC-AUC.

87.1% ROC-AUC 82.7% Accuracy 576-dim Fusion Vector Outperforms Unimodal Baselines
  • MedCLIP (Vision): 512-dim semantic embeddings from ultrasound images via a vision-language model pretrained on medical data.
  • TabNet (Tabular): Attention-based processing of clinical metadata — patient age, BI-RADS, tissue density, radiometric features.
  • Early Fusion: Concatenated 576-dim vector passed through a classification head.
  • Results: 82.7% Accuracy • 87.1% ROC-AUC — significantly outperforming unimodal baselines.
class CancerFusionModel(nn.Module): def __init__(self): self.medclip = MedCLIPModel() # 512-dim self.tabnet = TabNetEncoder() # 64-dim self.head = nn.Linear(576, 2) def forward(self, image, tabular): img = self.medclip(image) # [B, 512] tab = self.tabnet(tabular) # [B, 64] return self.head(torch.cat([img, tab], dim=1))
Engineering Decisions & What Didn't Work
  • Late fusion failed first. Initially trained image and tabular branches independently and combined predictions. Results were worse than either branch alone — the fusion was averaging noise, not learning interactions. Early fusion (concatenating 512+64 embeddings before the classification head) allowed cross-modal feature learning and recovered the gap.
  • ResNet50 dropped ROC-AUC by ~6 points vs MedCLIP. General vision models don't transfer well to medical imaging semantics. Domain-pretrained encoders are not optional for clinical AI tasks.
  • Class imbalance caused 94% false-negative rate before correction. Weighted cross-entropy loss fixed this at the cost of ~3% overall accuracy — the correct trade-off in a screening context.

Brain Tumor Classification
PyTorch • ViT • DINOv2 • Grad-CAM

Architecture benchmark on MRI data: Vision Transformer vs DINOv2 with Grad-CAM explainability. Built during Kontemporary internship.

ViT: 91.4%DINOv2: 93.8%Grad-CAMMRI
  • DINOv2-B: 93.8% accuracy — faster convergence, better tumor-boundary localization on Grad-CAM. Winner.
  • ViT-B/16: 91.4% accuracy — more epochs required, diffuse attention patterns on scan.
  • Inference: DINOv2 ~12ms/image vs ViT ~18ms/image on CPU — relevant for clinical deployment.
Key insight
  • ViT's fixed-grid patch encoding conflicted with MRI's variable-scale features. DINOv2's self-supervised pretraining transferred better despite never seeing MRI data.
