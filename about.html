My Story

I'm a Computer Science graduate from Landmark University with a sharp focus on applied machine learning and data engineering — specifically, making AI work outside controlled environments.

My thesis was the clearest demonstration of this. Rather than train another single-modality classifier on a clean benchmark, I designed a multimodal fusion system for breast cancer detection that combines ultrasound image embeddings (MedCLIP) with clinical and radiometric tabular data (TabNet) into a unified architecture. The result — 87.1% ROC-AUC — significantly outperformed unimodal baselines. That wasn't luck. It was an architectural decision made after understanding why existing approaches fell short.

During my internship at Kontemporary Konsulting Ltd, I was handed a task with no clean API, no structured data, and no playbook: extract polling unit results from all 36 Nigerian states. I architected a Selenium-based scraping system that navigated multi-layer dynamically rendered structures, validated every record, and loaded it into a clean queryable PostgreSQL database. 99.9% accuracy. 90% time reduction.

At TETFund, I replaced a manual report processing workflow with Python automation pipelines — handling messy institutional Excel files, correcting inconsistencies, and migrating legacy records into structured databases. Unglamorous, high-impact engineering that keeps organizations running.

I write code that solves specific real problems. I document what I build so others can maintain it. And I work well with imperfect data — because that's all there is in the real world.
